# Eye-donia — Brand Direction
**Artist:** Aidonia
**Product:** Sunglasses
**Brand Name:** Eye-donia
**Short Name:** Eye-donia (the pun is the whole point — never shorten it)

---

## Concept

The name is the brand. "Eye-donia" — it's already in his name, and the product category writes itself. Sunglasses for someone who moves through the world with the energy of the 4th generation: confident, seen, but always behind the lens. You wear these because your vision is different from everyone else's.

Not a fashion accessory. A declaration.

---

## Logo / Wordmark

- **"Eye-donia"** — the "Eye" portion slightly stylised: the dot of the "i" replaced with a minimal pupil mark or a tiny lens graphic
- Clean modern sans, medium weight — confident but not aggressive
- The hyphen is structural — "Eye" and "donia" are intentionally split to make the pun visible
- **"4th Genna Vision"** as the submark beneath the wordmark in small spaced caps
- Gold and black as the primary colourway for all brand assets

## Typography

- **Wordmark:** Clean geometric sans — Gilroy, Outfit, or similar. Medium weight, not bold
- **"4th Genna Vision":** Small caps, tracked wide, same family
- **Packaging body text:** Light weight, minimal, lots of white space

## AI Image Direction

**Hero shot:** Model wearing The 4th — matte black oversized square frames — standing in direct Jamaican sun. Shot from below eye level looking up slightly. City or hills in the background, softly out of focus. High contrast. The shadow the frames cast on the face is as important as the frames themselves.

**Product flat lay:** All four styles arranged on a clean white surface. Shot from directly above. Gold accents catching the light. Clean, editorial, like a lookbook opener.

**Close-up detail:** The "Eye-donia" wordmark engraved on the inner arm of the frame. Extreme macro. Gold on black.

**Lifestyle shot:** Subject silhouetted against a bright Kingston skyline, sun behind them, frames barely visible — just the outline. The viewer fills in the rest.

**Second lifestyle:** Subject in motion — not posing, walking through a market or down a street. The frames are incidental to their confidence, not the reason for it.

**Lighting rule:** Natural light always. Harsh midday sun preferred. No studio flash. The frames should earn their keep in real light.

## Tone & Voice

- Confident to the edge of quiet arrogance — but it's earned, not performed
- Never explains the pun — lets people discover it
- Speaks to people who were already going to stand out, with or without the glasses
- Short. Declarative. Final.
